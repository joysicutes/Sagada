Peaks of Sagada

Here are some places you can go if you would like mountains.

Mt. Ampacao

The highest peak in Sagada, offering a panoramic view of the town, nearby villages, and even the famous sea of clouds at sunrise.

It's one of the easier treks in Sagada, perfect for beginners who want rewarding views without a difficult climb.

View Map

Marlboro Hills

Famous for its breathtaking sunrise and sea of clouds. On clear mornings, you can even spot nearby mountain ranges like Mt. Pulag

Marlboro Hills offers rolling grassy hills perfect for photography, hiking, and serene nature walks, often compared to the landscapes of Batanes.

View Map

Kiltepan Peak

Once the most popular sunrise viewpoint in Sagada, known for the legendary “sea of clouds” scene.

Though it's now regulated due to preservation efforts, it remains an iconic part of Sagada's mountain charm.

View Map

Mt. Polis

Scenic mountain connecting Sagada and Bontoc, offering lush pine forests and cool winds.

Great for travelers who enjoy road trips or cycling, it's part of the scenic route with postcard-perfect views.

View Map

Blue Soil Hills

The Blue Soil Hills, located near Marlboro Hills, are known for their unique bluish tint that changes color depending on sunlight. The soil's color comes from its high copper sulfate content.

It's a rare natural wonder, a scenic, easy hike from Marlboro Hills and a favorite spot for photography and nature walks.

View Map

The Hanging Coffins

One of Sagada's most famous and culturally significant attractions! The Hanging Coffins are an ancient and unique burial tradition of the indigenous Kankanaey people.

The primary belief is that placing the deceased high up on the cliffs brings their spirits closer to the ancestral world and the gods.

View Map

The Refreshing Side of Sagada

Discover Sagada's refreshing charm through its crystal-clear waterfalls, tranquil streams, and nature's soothing embrace.

Bomod-ok Falls

This is arguably the most famous and majestic waterfall in Sagada, cascading about 200 feet into a large, serene pool at the bottom.

he trek to the falls is an adventure in itself, taking a few hours round-trip. You'll pass through picturesque rice terraces and local communities, which makes the refreshing swim in the cool waters at the base of the falls a well-earned reward.

View Map

Bokong Waterfalls

Known as the "small falls," this cascade is a more accessible alternative, only about a 30-minute walk from the town center. It stands about 20 feet high and flows into a deep natural pool.

This is a favorite spot for swimming and relaxation, as the water is noted to be less cold than other falls in the area. It's often included in the Eco Valley Tour.

View Map

Pongas Falls

This is a less-traveled but equally majestic attraction, often described as a 40-meter-high twin-like waterfall.

The trek to Pongas Falls typically takes one to two hours, leading you through lush landscapes and scenic trails in the Ankileng village.

View Map

Sumaguing-Lumiang Cave Connection

Sagada is built on a massive network of limestone caves, which contain fascinating underground streams and pools.

The famous Cave Connection tour links the Lumiang and Sumaguing caves, where the adventure involves traversing tiny tunnels, crawling over rocks, and swimming through underground pools. The smaller Sumaguing Cave also features its own underground pools and streams amidst remarkable rock formations.

View Map

Obwa Blue Lagoon

This hidden spot is a unique oasis reached by a short hike that passes through the enchanting Obwa Terraces and its cascading waterfalls.

It features a pristine pool where visitors can take a refreshing dip and enjoy the serene, lush surroundings.

View Map

Lake Danum

While not an attraction for swimming, this tranquil lake is a popular natural body of water in Sagada.

It is best known as a spectacular spot for watching the sunset, where the late afternoon light casts golden hues over the water and the surrounding pine-covered mountains.

View Map

Sagada Specialties

Sagada's cuisine is a hearty blend of indigenous Cordilleran dishes and unique local specialties.

Pinikpikan

A comforting chicken soup traditionally cooked with Etag (smoked and salted pork) and fresh ginger. It's known for its smoky aroma and deeply flavorful, warming broth, perfect for the cool mountain climate.

Visit Restaurant

Etag

Sagada's indigenous cured meat. It is a slab of pork that is heavily salted and then aged, often by smoking or air-drying. It imparts a distinct, smoky, and salty flavor to stews and vegetable dishes.

Visit Restaurant

Sagada Arabica Coffee

The high-altitude, cool climate of the Mountain Province is ideal for growing Arabica beans. Sagada coffee is known for its strong, robust flavor and is the perfect warm drink to accompany your meals.

Visit Restaurant

History Spots

Discover Sagada's cultural heritage, weaving and local history.

Ganduyan Museum

This small, treasured museum showcases a variety of historic items, including intricate sculptures, ancient jewelry, kitchenware, textiles, and war weapons, offering a deeper insight into Igorot beliefs and practices. Visitors often praise the experience for the detailed and witty explanations provided by the curator/owner.

View Museum

Church of St. Mary the Virgin

A significant historical and religious landmark known for its distinct architecture and deep roots in the community. It stands as a central place of worship and a reflection of the region's cultural intersection with Western influence.

View Church

Sagada Weaving

Sagada Weaving is a must-visit souvenir shop in the picturesque Sagada, Philippines. It showcases the traditional weaving artistry of the Mountain Province, offering a diverse selection of handwoven textiles such as blankets and scarves crafted by local artisans
