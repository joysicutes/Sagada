Overview of Sagada

Nestled high in the Cordillera Mountains of the Philippines, Sagada is a peaceful mountain town located at Mountain Province, known for its breathtaking landscapes, cool climate, and rich cultural heritage. Famous for its hanging coffins, limestone caves, waterfalls, and sea of clouds, Sagada offers both adventure and serenity for travelers seeking to connect with nature and local tradition.

People

Beyond its stunning views, Sagada is home to the Igorot people, whose traditions of mountain farming, weaving, and communal living reflect a deep respect for the land. The community in Sagada is known for its strong sense of unity, simplicity, and respect for tradition.

Horizons

Sagada is a haven of towering mountains, lush pine forests, and rolling sea of clouds. Mist drifts through Echo Valley, where ancient hanging coffins cling to limestone cliffs. Waterfalls cascade into crystal pools, caves glow with adventure, and sunrise at Kiltepan Viewpoint paints the sky in gold. Every corner of Sagada feels calm, timeless, and close to nature, a perfect escape for the soul.

Seasons of Sagada

Plan your trip to Sagada, where it offers breathtaking beauty year-round, with each season revealing its own unique charm amidst the mountains.

Peak Season

November-February

Cool, clear weather perfect for hiking and sightseeing.

Low Season

May-October

Warmer and more affordable, but watch out for rain and typhoons.

Busy Periods

December & Holy Week

Expect larger crowds; trails and accommodations may be busier.

Cultural Festivals

Sagada's cultural festivals celebrate its rich traditions, honoring harvests, ancestry, and the enduring spirit of the Kankanaey people.

Begnas Festival

October-January

A thanksgiving for the rice harvest, celebrating community and gratitude.

Panag-etag Festival

January 30 - February 2

Honors local traditions and rituals with cultural dances and ceremonies.

Bonfire Festival

December 30 - January 1

A lively holiday celebration for locals and visitors alike with music and warmth.
